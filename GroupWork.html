What you will be able to do by the end
Explain how networks communicate, including types, components, and connection methods
Set up a wireless router and connect devices to the internet securely
Understand why standards and protocols matter for devices to communicate with each other
Read and explain IP addresses, including both IPv4 and IPv6
Configure a DHCP server to assign IP addresses automatically
Explain how routers connect separate networks together
Build a fully connected Local Area Network (LAN) from scratch
Use tools like ping, ipconfig, and traceroute to test and fix network issues

When and how
The standup happens at the start of every class and lasts 15 minutes. A different Working Group member leads it each day, which means every learner gets to practice facilitating before this module ends. The leader that day logs the notes in the shared document.
Three questions every member answers
Question 1
What did you complete yesterday?
Question 2
What will you focus on today?
Question 3
Are there any blockers stopping your progress?
Why this matters
This is the exact standup format used in real cybersecurity and tech teams every single day. Practicing it now means you walk into your first job already knowing how to communicate your progress clearly and professionally. It is not a performance, it is a habit you are building starting today.
How to succeed in this module
Communicate often and openly with your Working Group, and do not wait until you are completely stuck
Take notes during walkthroughs and demos, because you will need them when doing the labs independently
Ask for help early and offer support to teammates who are behind
Plan your day at the start of each session and know exactly which lesson or lab you are targeting
Use the shared documentation and checklists to track what is done and what still needs attention
Hold each other accountable, because a strong team does not leave anyone behind
Collaborate on challenges but always submit your own Proof of Completion (POC)
Think of each task as a real-world ticket, not just an assignment, and it will change how seriously you approach it
